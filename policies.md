# Policies 

## National Policies 

These are policies created and implemented by the federal government. They usually shape the largest disaster-response systems that include nationwide funding, recovery coordination, and mitigation programs.  Because they typically rely on federal declarations, appovals, and agency processes, support can sometimes be broader in scale but slower to reach local communities. 

* Robert T. Stafford Disaster Relief and Emegency Assistance Act of 1988
    - Serves as the main legal foundation for federal disaster aid and helps to determine when states and communities are allowed to access major recovery assistance 
* Post-Katina Emergency Management reform Act of 2006
    - Reorganized FEMA following Hurricane Katrina and strengthened federal preparedness and coordination capacity duing major natural disasters 
* National Disaster recovery Framework (NDRF)
    - Provides the overall structure for long-term disater recovery so that states and communities can move from response into organized rebuilding following a natural disaster 
* Recovery Support Functions (RSFs)
    - Breaks recovery into major sectors such as housing, infrastructure, health, and economy
* Emergency Management Assistance Compact (EMAC)
    - Allows states to send personnel, equipment, and support one another to help response speed and recovery when one state is overwhelmed
* Hazard Mitigation Grant Program (HMGP)
    - Funds projects after disasters that will reduce future risk, helping communities rebuild in ways that improve long-term recovey capacity 
* Building Resilient INfrastructue and Communities (BRIC)
    - Suppots pre-disaster resilience investments so that states adn localities can reduce the damage and shorten future recovery timelines 
* Flood Mitigation Assistance (FMA)
    - Funds flod-risk reduction projects fo NFIP-insured properties, helping lower repeat losses in high-risk areas
    - FMA Swift Current 
    - Speeds up mitigation funding after major floods so repetitive-loss properties can reduce risk sooner rather than waiting for a longe grant process
* National Flood Insurance Progam (NFIP)
    - Provides federally backed flood insurance and connects insurance access with floodplain management standards that shape local recovery 
    - Risk rating 2.0
    - Updates NFIP pricing to refelct property-level flood risk more directly, which changes how flood exposure is piced across communities
* Community Rating Sytem (CRS)
    - Rewads communities that go beyond the minimum floodplain standards by reducing flood insurance premiums for residents
* Disaster ecovery Reform Act of 2018 
    - Shifted federal policy further toward mitigation and resilience, reinforcing the idea that faster recovery depends on planning before disasters happen 

### State Policies 

These are policies created by state government level to espond to each state's individual risks, budget capacity, and recovery priorities.  They can shape how quickly states pepare for disasters, distribute aid, manage insurance makets, and support the rebuilding pocess beyong what federal pograms and policies can provide. 

* Florida Emergency Management Act (Ch 252, Florida Statutes)
    - Established Florida's emergency management system and gives the state and local governments authority to coordinate disaster response and recovery  
* My Safe Florida Home Pogram
    - Helps homewoners strengthen homes against hurricanes through inspections and grants to reduce damage and improve household ecovery after storms 
* Florida Huricana Catastrophe Fund (FHCF)
    - Provides reimbursement support to insurers afte catastrophic hurricane losses, helping stabilize Florida's property insurance market after major disasters 
* California FAIR Plan
    -  Acts as a backup coverage option for residents and businesses that cannot get insurance in the regular market, especially in high-risk wildfire areas
* Proposition 103 (California, 1988)
    - Created California's modern insurance rate-regulation framework, shaping how affordability and insurer oversight affect recovery in risk-prone areas 
* California Sustainable Insurance Strategy 
    - Tries to expand insurance availability in wildfire-distressed areas while reducing reliance on the FAIR plan  
* Safer fom Wildfires
    - Links wildfire mitigation actions by homeowners and communities to insurance incentives, encouraging prevention before losses occur

### Local Policies 

These are policies created by counties, cities, and local governments to address risks and recovery at a community level.  They are often the most directly connected to what esidents experience on the ground such as zoning, floodplain management, evacuation planning, rebuilding rules, and local resilience projects. 

* FEMA-appoved Local Hazard Mitigation Plans
    - Helps communities identify local isks and prioritize projects that reduce future losses, which directly supports faster and more stable recoveries 
* Community Rating System paticipation 
    - Allows local governments to lower residents' flood insurance costs by adopting stonger floodplain management and resilience measures
* Miami-Dade Sea Level rise Strategy 
    - Focuses on adaptation planning for rising sea levels and coastal flooding, helping a high-risk region reduce long-term recovery strain 
* Harris County Home Buyout / Voluntary Buyout Program 
    - Reduces repeated flood losses by moving residents out of the high-risk floodplain areas instead of repeatedly rebuilding in place 
* New Orleans Hazard Mitigation Strategy 
    - Prioritizes long-term local actions that reduce losses from hazards and strengthen recovery across infrastructure and communities 
* New Orleans stormwater management code 
    - Uses local building and development standards to educe unoff and flooding isk, which can lessen future ecovery budens
* Los Angeles wildfire rebuilding executive orders (2025)
    - Accelerates rebuilding after wildfie while also trying to imrpvoe resilience against futue fire-related losses 

### Private/Insurance Policies

These policies are contained more within the private sector or insurance systems, even if they can be influenced by state and federal regulations.  They affect how individuals, homeowners, and businesses recover financially after disasters by shaping avilability of coverage, premiums, claims, and access to potection in high-risk areas.

* Citizens Property Insurance Corporation 
    - Serves as Florida's insurer of last resort, helping homeowners maintian coverage when the private market pulls back after repeated losses
* Private homeowners and commercial property insurance 
    - Dtermines how quickly households and businesses can repair, reopen, and financially recover after a natural disaster 
* State insurer-of-last-resort programs 
    - Provides backup insurance access in high-risk markets when private carriers reduce availability o exit altogether
* State catastrophe backstops/reinsurance 
    - Help insurers absorb extreme losses after major events, reducing the chance of wider market instability following natural disasters 
* Mitigation-linked insurance disconts 
    - Encouages households to harden homes befoe disaters by tying resilience upgrades to possible premium savings or improved insurability
* Property Insurance Clearinghouse (FL)
    - Helps move consumes from Citizens into private-market options when avialable, which can improve market flexibility after disasters
